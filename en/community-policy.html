Tadak Community Policy

UGC policy and reporting or enforcement standards

Published: 2026-03-06 Updated: 2026-03-06

Quick Summary

  • The community feed shows typing-practice UGC that users choose to share publicly.
  • Profanity, abusive content, sexual content, illegal material, and rights-infringing content are not allowed.
  • Posts may be temporarily hidden after repeated reports or when moderation review is required, and enforcement stages may follow.

1. Scope and Definitions

  • This document applies to the Tadak public UGC area called the community feed.
  • `Public` means a user has chosen to expose a writing to the community feed.
  • `Hidden` means public exposure has been stopped by user choice or by moderation action.
  • `Hide author` is a personal control that stops a specific author's public posts from appearing in your own feed. It is separate from moderation enforcement.
  • `Report` means a policy concern submitted about a public post or user, and `enforcement` means the operational action taken after review.

2. Posting Eligibility and Public Sharing

  • Guest accounts cannot publish publicly in the community feed. Account linking is required before a post can be shared publicly.
  • When you save a writing as public, other users may see it in the community feed.
  • You may switch your own writing back to private, but already-filed reports or moderation records may still be handled separately.

3. Prohibited Content

  • Profanity, abuse, harassment, hateful expression, humiliation, or threats
  • Sexual or explicit content, pornography, objectification, or graphic sexual wording
  • Illegal content, copyright infringement, impersonation, or other rights violations
  • Personal data exposure, contact harvesting, promotional spam, or repeated flooding
  • Unauthorized automation, abnormal traffic, or interference with service operation

The current app blocks some profanity and sexual wording before public upload using keyword-based filters. Passing a pre-upload check does not prevent later reporting or enforcement.

4. How Reports Are Submitted

  • Users can submit both post reports and user reports inside the community feed.
  • Post-report data may include the reporter identifier, reported post identifier, report reason, and submission timestamp.
  • User-report data may include the reporter identifier, reported user identifier, related post identifier, report reason, submission timestamp, and target snapshot.
  • If follow-up or appeal is needed, contact omilsoft@gmail.com.

4-1. Hide or Block Author

  • You can hide a specific author so that the author's posts no longer appear in your own community feed.
  • Hiding an author is a viewer-side control only and does not mean the author has been moderated or sanctioned by OMILSOFT.
  • You can manage and unhide authors from the in-app hidden-author management list.

5. Temporary Hiding Standard

  • Repeated reports may trigger temporary hiding to protect users and enable review.
  • Under the current operational standard, a report count of 3 or more is one temporary-hide trigger.
  • Temporary hiding may also be applied for legal risk, clearly prohibited content, safety concerns, or moderator judgment.

6. Enforcement Stages

  • `normal`: post stays visible
  • `temporary hidden`: public visibility is blocked during review
  • `on hold`: a report has been received and more review or evidence is needed
  • `delete candidate`: a serious violation appears confirmed or deletion or stronger restriction is under review
  • Additional measures may include limiting public sharing, keeping a post hidden, restoring a post, or restricting related features.

7. Handling Principles

  • User safety and rights protection come first.
  • We consider policy language, report content, repetition, clarity, and severity together.
  • We try to limit visibility only to the extent necessary and may keep operational review logs.
  • Repeated or escalating violations can lead to stronger action.

8. Appeals and Contact Channel

  • Questions about hidden posts or moderation actions can be sent to the support email.
  • Please include account-identifying details, the post title or report timing, and your request so that we can review it efficiently.
  • Support, account deletion, privacy, and moderation inquiries all use the same email: omilsoft@gmail.com.
